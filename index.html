CAS Physical Intelligence Team

We are the Physical Intelligence Team at the Chinese Academy of Sciences (CAS). Our mission is to build intelligent, reliable, and practically deployable robots by bridging geometry, physics, and learning-based control.

Our research interests include embodied intelligence, physical & spatial intelligence, robotic manipulation and navigation, world modeling, neural simulators, and learning with physical priors. We aim to develop systems that generalize across tasks and environments, and can be validated in real-world industrial scenarios.

PI / Leadership

Kai Xu (徐凯) is a Professor at the Institute of AI for Industries (IAII), Chinese Academy of Sciences. Before joining IAII, he was a professor at National University of Defense Technology (NUDT). He used to be an Adjunct Professor of Simon Fraser University. He earned his Ph.D in computer science in 2011 from NUDT. From 2008 to 2010, he worked as a visiting PhD in the GrUVi Lab of Simon Fraser University, supervised by Richard (Hao) Zhang. During 2012-2014, he conducted postdoctoral research at the VCC@SIAT, working with Baoquan Chen. During 2017-2018, he visited the Vision and Robotics Group at Princeton University, working with Thomas Funkhouser and Szymon Rusinkiewicz. Here is Kai's CV.

Kai is leading the PAI Lab @ IAII and co-leading the iGRAPE Lab @ NUDT, which conduct research in the areas of computer graphics, embodied intelligence and physical & spatial intelligence. The current directions of interest include world modeling, neural simulator and robotic manipulation & navigation, etc. For more details, please refer to our publications.

Research

We study physical intelligence: integrating physical priors with data-driven methods to enable robust perception–decision–control loops under contact-rich interactions and deformation.
